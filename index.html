Willem Leterme

Willem Leterme performs research to enhance the transition to a renewable-driven carbon-free power system. He is currently a senior researcher associated with the University of Leuven and the EnergyVille research center, where he leads a small team of post-doctoral researchers and PhD students. Previously, he has been a post-doc at KU Leuven and a visiting researcher at Imperial College London. His PhD has received funding from FWO-flanders. He has received the KVBE/SRBE award for his PhD thesis.

Currently, his research focuses on enhancing automated decision making processes for future power systems primarily focusing on fast transient phenomena, such as identification of short-circuit faults and decisions involving switching transients. In this field, his aim is to develop new algorithms that allow for taking actions on a millisecond time-basis as well as relating the impact of these actions to operation and planning on a minute to hourly basis. To this end, he combines his expertise on power systems with elements from decision theory, statistical inference and machine learning to balance computational complexity while guaranteeing an adequate level of accuracy.
